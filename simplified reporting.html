Landslide Types

click here for detailed version

  • Landslide Type: Flow

    In a flow, distributed (fluid-like) movement of a mass of rock or soil may proceed slowly or swiftly. Flows of mud and debris commonly resemble rivers of rapidly moving wet concrete.

  • Landslide Type: Fall

    A fall occurs when a mass of any size detaches from a steep slope or cliff and descends mostly through the air by freefall, bouncing, or rolling.

  • Landslide Type: Topple

    A topple consists of the forward rotation or tilting of a column of rock or soil.

  • Landslide Type: Rotational

    In rotational sliding, movement is along a spoon-shaped or cylindrical surface so that movement at the top (head) of the slide appears to be mostly downward and the ground surface at the head may tilt backward, into the hillside.

  • Landslide Type: Translational

    In translational sliding, movement occurs along a more or less planar surface that commonly is almost parallel to the hillside.

  • Landslide Type: Spread.jpg

    In a spread, lateral movement causes the ground to pull apart, with resulting formation of a series of more or less parallel fractures. Spreading may occur on gentle slopes.

  • Landslide Type: Avalanche

    An avalanche is an extremely rapid flow-like or sheet-like movement of a large, crumbling mass of rock down a steep mountainside or over a cliff.
